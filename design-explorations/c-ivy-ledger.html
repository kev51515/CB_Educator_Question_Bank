06 Test Runner — calm regardless of direction
Section 1, Module 2: Reading and WritingDirections ▾
27:14

For decades, marine biologists assessed the health of coral reefs almost entirely by sight, counting fish and measuring coral cover along transect lines. Yet reefs are extraordinarily loud places: snapping shrimp crackle like static, parrotfish rasp at coral, and damselfish drum to defend their territories. Ecologist Tim Gordon and colleagues recorded soundscapes along the Great Barrier Reef and found that degraded reefs were measurably quieter and less acoustically varied than healthy ones. When the team played recordings of healthy reefs through underwater speakers at damaged sites, juvenile fish settled there at twice the usual rate. The finding suggests that sound is not merely a symptom of a reef's condition but an active cue that shapes its recovery.
