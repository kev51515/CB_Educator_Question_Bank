Q1

While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes: 

  • On January 3, 1959, Alaska became the 49th state to join the US.        
  • On August 21, 1959, Hawaii became the 50th state to join the US.        
  • A new 50-star US flag was unveiled the same day.

The student wants to emphasize the order in which Alaska and Hawaii became US states. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

  1. A.

    Alaska, the 49th US state, became a state several months before Hawaii, the 50th state, did.

  2. B.

    On August 21, 1959, a new 50-star US flag was unveiled.

  3. C.

    The 49th and 50th states to join the US did so in the same year.

  4. D.

    The same day that Hawaii became a US state—August 21, 1959—a new US flag was unveiled.

  • This behavior serves to startle predators.
  • Black dragonfish emit a steady red light.
  • This behavior helps them locate prey in deep waters.

    Both jellyfish and black dragonfish are organisms that emit light, which is produced by chemical reactions in these organisms’ cells.

  2. B.

    Black dragonfish emit a steady red light, which helps them locate prey in deep waters.

  3. C.

    Bioluminescence, the emission of light by living organisms, results from chemical reactions in organisms’ cells. 

  4. D.

    Jellyfish emit light to startle predators, whereas black dragonfish do so to locate prey.

    Q9

    While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:

    • The calendar used by most of the world (the Gregorian calendar) has 365 days.
    • Because 365 days can’t be divided evenly by 7 (the number of days in a week), calendar dates fall on a different day of the week each year.
    • The Hanke-Henry permanent calendar, developed as an alternative to the Gregorian calendar, has 364 days.
    • Because 364 can be divided evenly by 7, calendar dates fall on the same day of the week each year, which supports more predictable scheduling.

    The student wants to explain an advantage of the Hanke-Henry calendar. Which choice most effectively uses relevant information from the notes to accomplish this goal?

    1. A.

      The Gregorian calendar has 365 days, which is one day longer than the Hanke-Henry permanent calendar.

    2. B.

      Adopting the Hanke-Henry permanent calendar would help solve a problem with the Gregorian calendar.

    3. C.

      Designed so calendar dates would occur on the same day of the week each year, the Hanke-Henry calendar supports more predictable scheduling than does the Gregorian calendar.

    4. D.

      The Hanke-Henry permanent calendar was developed as an alternative to the Gregorian calendar, which is currently the most-used calendar in the world.
