Substituting 8 for f(32) in the equation g(32)=f(32)+8 yields g(32)=8+8, or g(32)=16. By the factor theorem, if g(24)=0 and g(30)=0, then g(x)=k(x-24)(x-30), where k is a constant. Since g(32)=16, substituting 32 for x and 16 for g(x) in the equation g(x)=k(x-24)(x-30) yields 16=k(32-24)(32-30), or 16=k(8)(2), which is equivalent to 16=16k. Dividing both sides of this equation by 16 yields 1=k. Substituting 1 for k in the equation g(x)=k(x-24)(x-30) yields g(x)=(x-24)(x-30). Since g(x)=f(x)+8, it follows that g(x)-8=f(x). Subtracting 8 from both sides of the equation g(x)=(x-24)(x-30) yields g(x)-8=(x-24)(x-30)-8. Substituting f(x) for g(x)-8 in this equation yields f(x)=(x-24)(x-30)-8.
